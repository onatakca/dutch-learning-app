# Wikibooks Dutch Language Course Structure

This document outlines the structure of the open-source Dutch language course from Wikibooks, to be used as a reference for populating the Dutch Learning App.

## Course Overview
The course is divided into three main levels:
1.  **Beginner Level**: Focuses on basic conversation, introduction to grammar, simple sentences, and building a vocabulary of ~1000 terms.
2.  **Intermediate Level**: Focuses on complex sentences, syntax, modal verbs, and past tenses.
3.  **Advanced Level**: Deepens command of the language with special topics like prepositions and adverbs.

---

## Beginner Level
*Goal: Form simple sentences and understand basic grammar.*

### Lesson 1: The Basics
*   **Grammar**:
    *   Personal Pronouns (Subject & Object)
    *   The Verb *Zijn* (To Be) - Present Tense
    *   Polite vs. Familiar forms (*U* vs. *Jij*)
*   **Vocabulary**: Greetings, Introductions.
*   **Culture**: N/A

### Lesson 2: Nouns & Articles
*   **Grammar**:
    *   Gender of Nouns (*De* vs. *Het*)
    *   Indefinite Article (*Een*)
    *   Plural of Nouns (-en, -s)
*   **Vocabulary**: Common objects, family members.

### Lesson 3: Questions & Numbers
*   **Grammar**:
    *   Word Order in Questions (Inversion)
    *   Interrogatives (Wie, Wat, Waar, etc.)
*   **Vocabulary**:
    *   Numbers 1-100
    *   Telling Time
    *   Colors

### Lesson 4: Adjectives & Pronouns
*   **Grammar**:
    *   Adjective Endings (The *e* rule)
    *   Possessive Pronouns (Mijn, Jouw, etc.)
    *   Demonstrative Pronouns (Deze, Die, Dit, Dat)

---

## Intermediate Level
*Goal: Handle complex sentences and past tenses.*

### Lesson 5: Verbs & Tenses
*   **Grammar**:
    *   Modal Verbs (*Kunnen, Mogen, Willen*, etc.)
    *   Future Tense (*Zullen*)
    *   Past Tense (Imperfectum & Perfectum)

### Lesson 6: Word Order & Syntax
*   **Grammar**:
    *   Separable Verbs
    *   Conjunctions (En, Maar, Want vs. Omdat, Als)
    *   The Passive Voice

---

## Advanced Level
*Goal: Nuance and specific complex topics.*

### Topics
*   **Adverbs**: Formation and usage.
*   **Prepositions**: Detailed usage of *in, op, bij, aan*, etc.
*   **Pronominal Adverbs**: *Er* + Preposition (e.g., *Erop, Daarin*).
